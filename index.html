About The Chef

History

From Large-Scale Service to Limited Fine Dining

The Burn Room is rooted in heritage hospitality and rebuilt around a modern, invitation-led format. The work moved from high-volume systems into an intimate service model where detail, pacing, and execution define the experience.

Guided by the quiet discipline of Japanese influence and sharpened in professional kitchens, The Burn Room combines precision technique with fearless creativity — including fermentation, fire, and molecular gastronomy where it serves the dish, never the ego.

Some courses require up to 72 hours of preparation. Even seemingly simple plates are engineered for depth, freshness, and timing. In one menu, the pink broth with dumplings was built over 48 hours while preserving clarity and lift.

Ingredients are sourced with intent. There are mornings that begin with sourcing runs from Kochi to Idukki, because quality is treated as a decision, not a slogan. The Burn Room is chef-led, detail-obsessed, and designed to feel rare without feeling distant.
